Design-led  Incubation

Our interest lays in steering startups to create design-integrated businesses that tackle local socioeconomic issues in overlapping sectors including Healthcare, Transport, Agriculture, Housing, Safety, Water, Energy and Waste Management using Design.

Why our business incubator stands out

With our focus on building design-integrated businesses, we adopt a design-led approach by guiding our startup companies to:

• Build products or services that are consumer-focused.
• Use design as an essential perspective in the product/service development process, from start to finish.
• Embed design as a strategic tool in their management operations.
• Implement design as a differentiator for their products/services.

Application Guide

Kindly read this guide before you begin your application by submitting a proposal

01

Enrolment Process

Our incubator programme is enrolled for by submitting a brief proposal on the design business venture (project) the candidate intends to pursue. Shortlisted candidates are then interviewed and successful candidates are enrolled into our incubator program.

02

Applicant's Background

Our incubator is open to applicants from all education backgrounds. However, candidates with some experience in design practice are likely to be at a competitive advantage since they are more likely to demonstrate healthy dose of Design Thinking.

03

Proposal Guidelines

The proposed venture should demonstrate the role of design (with focus on Interaction, Package, Product, Brand, Service & Industrial design) in tackling local socioeconomic problems.

Preferrably, the proposed venture (project) should be culturally relevant and also foster cross-disciplinary cooperation.

04

Considerable Sectors

We are keen on using our specified areas of design in creating value in cross-disciplinary sectors such as:
• Healthcare — Maternal mortality, Fitness, Disease control, etc.
• Agriculture — Food security, Sustainable Agriculture, etc.
• Environmental Sustainability — Waste management, Climate change, Sanitation, Marine conservation, etc.
